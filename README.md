# NovaRetail_Customer_Behavior_Correlation_Analysis
🛒 NovaRetail+ — Customer Behavior Correlation Analysis
📌 Project Overview

NovaRetail+ is a large e-commerce platform operating across Latin America.

For the end of 2024, the Growth & Retention team wanted to understand:

Which customer behavior factors are most strongly associated with annual revenue generation?

This project performs a structured exploratory and correlational analysis using customer behavioral, demographic, and monetization data.

⚠️ Important: This is a correlational analysis. Correlation does not imply causation.

🎯 Objectives

The goal of this project is to:

Identify relationships between customer behavior and annual revenue.
Detect patterns associated with high-value customers.
Explore behavioral differences between customer segments.
Apply multiple correlation techniques depending on variable type.
Generate responsible business insights supported by statistical evidence.
📂 Dataset

Dataset used:

novaretail_comportamiento_clientes_2024.csv

The dataset contains 15,000 customer records and includes:

Column	Description
id_cliente	Unique customer identifier
edad	Customer age
nivel_ingreso	Estimated annual customer income
visitas_mes	Monthly visits to the platform
compras_mes	Monthly purchases
gasto_publicidad_dirigida	Advertising spend assigned to the user
satisfaccion	Customer satisfaction score (1–5)
miembro_premium	Premium membership status (0/1)
abandono	Customer churn indicator (0/1)
tipo_dispositivo	Device type used by the customer
region	Geographic region
ingreso_anual	Annual revenue generated by the customer

🎯 Main KPI analyzed:

📊 Key Findings
📈 Purchases vs Annual Revenue
Strong positive correlation (~0.97)
Customers with more purchases tend to generate significantly higher annual revenue.
Possible collinearity detected between both variables.
📊 Visits vs Annual Revenue
Moderate positive correlation (~0.33)
Higher platform activity tends to associate with higher revenue, although with high variability.
📉 Advertising Spend vs Visits
Moderate positive relationship (~0.58)
Users with higher advertising investment tend to show more visits.
⭐ Premium Membership vs Revenue
Weak positive relationship (~0.09)
Premium users generate slightly higher revenue, but the effect size is limited.
🌎 Device Type vs Region
Cramér’s V (~0.01)
No meaningful association detected between geographic region and device type.
💡 Business Insights
Purchase frequency appears to be one of the strongest indicators of customer value.
Customer visits alone are not sufficient to explain revenue generation.
Advertising campaigns may increase traffic, but not necessarily proportional revenue.
Premium membership alone does not strongly predict customer value.
Customer behavior segmentation could improve targeting and retention strategies.
⚠️ Limitations
Correlation does not imply causation.
Some variables may present strong collinearity.
External factors such as product pricing, discounts, or seasonality were not included.
Results are exploratory and should not be interpreted as predictive models.
